# Comprehensive Design Audit: Typography, Structure, Resonance

Audit typography as a system of "jobs"—each element must fulfill a functional mandate from attention capture to frictionless reading. Failure in one job increases cognitive load and can cause abandonment.

## Typographic Job Matrix

| Category | Objective | Metrics | Scanning role |
|----------|-----------|---------|----------------|
| **Display** | Catch attention, evoke resonance | Interest, gut feeling, memorability | Visual hook; entry point |
| **Body** | Clear, comfortable information | Legibility, comfort, "gets out of the way" | Sustained reading, processing |

Both must harmonize into one hierarchy that guides the eye.

## Pillar I: Body Text Legibility

**Method: "Break It and Fix It"**—push a setting to an obviously broken extreme, then adjust to the "limits of okay" for that context.

**Four critical decisions:**

1. **Font (sturdiness):** Typographic color (gray value) and "active texture"—sturdy shapes, not muddy gray.
2. **Size (legibility limit):** Comfortable at standard viewing distance; test boundaries.
3. **Measure/line length:** Too narrow = frantic rhythm; too wide = eye loses return to left margin. Ensure natural tracking.
4. **Leading (breathability):** Narrower measure → tighter leading; wider measure → looser leading so the eye can follow to the next line. Prevent lines merging into a wall.

## Pillar II: Display Text and Shape Harmony

Display text is judged by interest and resonance; it must share **Visual DNA** with body text to avoid fragmentation.

**Shape compatibility audit:**

- **Stems:** Are display stems slanted or straight? Same structural backbone as body?
- **Arcs and joins:** Shared sharpness or similar curves where shapes meet?
- **Positive/negative space:** Compare counters (voids in 'a', 'e', 'o') with word space and line spacing—do volumes feel equal in weight?
- **Weight benchmarking:** Thinnest part of display serif vs body stems; heavy part of display vs body x-height.

## Pillar III: Structural Audit (Alignment and Proximity)

- **Strong alignment axis:** Find the primary axis. Audit for **visual** alignment—elements can snap to grid but still look "off." Fix misaligned pairs.
- **Proximity:** Headings must associate with their content blocks, not with each other. Related items closer than unrelated. Avoid "murkiness."
- **Edge tension:** Elements placed closer to page/screen edges than to each other cause mess. Tighten internal proximity.

## Pillar IV: Resonance and Subjectivity

- **Adjective mapping:** Describe gut feeling beyond "good/bad"—elegant, trustworthy, casual, official. Use thesaurus or emojis.
- **Type designer language:** Use how font creators describe their work to build critique vocabulary.
- **Competitive benchmarking:** Compare to similar subject matter—familiar (expected) vs memorably unexpected.
- **Resources:** Behance, Fonts in Use (application); I Love Typography, Letterform Archive (context). Peer feedback for alignment of intent and experience.

## Diagnostic Toolkit

- **Squint test:** Unreadable words → judge volumes of space and typographic color without content distraction.
- **Inversion:** Turn layout upside down to judge weight, alignment, and balance without reading.
- **Distance study:** Step back; what is first, second, third? Match to strategic intent.
- **Toggle/side-by-side:** Never audit one solution alone; compare variations for balance and "correctness."

Growth comes from repeated application of these diagnostic exercises.
